Exploring the World of Literature

Classic novels have had a profound impact on literature by shaping the themes, narratives and character archetypes that continue to influence modern storytelling. For instance, Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen exploreclass and social dynamics, while 1984 by George Orwell delves into the chilling of totalitarianism and surveillance. In To Kill a Mockingbird, Harper Lee addresses racial injustice and moral growth. Meanwhile, Moby-Dick by Herman Melville examines the obession with revenge and The Great Gatsby by F.Scott Fitzgerald critiques the pursuit of the American Dream. These works have not only provided enduring literary value but have also sparked critical discussions and inspired countless adaptions. Here are the classic novels and their authors in an ordered list:

  1. Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen
  2. 1984 by George Orwell
  3. To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee
  4. Moby-Dick by Herman Melville
  5. The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald
